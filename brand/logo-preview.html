02 — Exploration
Logo directions explored
Three sharply differentiated concepts, each rooted in a different visual logic and brand interpretation.
Selected
Concept A
The Rising Nodes
Three ascending data points connected by a gradient trajectory line. Each node grows in size — navy (foundation), teal (momentum), gold (achievement). A geometric metaphor for measured, intelligent progress.
Selected — most distinctive and strategically precise
Concept B
The Signal R
A bold geometric "R" where the descending leg is inverted to rise — a gold diagonal slash where a standard typeface would fall. The lettermark encodes "Rise" literally in the R's anatomy. "IQ" completes it in teal.
Not selected — strong concept but R lettermarks lack distinction at scale
Concept C
The Band Dial
A precision measurement dial with its needle at maximum — directly referencing IQ scoring, IELTS band targets, and the product's core function of tracking progress toward a goal. Clean, literal, and purposeful.
Not selected — gauge metaphor is overused in edtech/HR categories
03 — Direction
Why "The Rising Nodes"
The selected concept wins on every strategic dimension that matters for a premium, AI-native learning platform.
Creative Director's Rationale
A mark that earns its meaning
Most logos try to illustrate what a product does. The Rising Nodes works differently — it encodes what RiseIQ believes: that intelligence is measurable, progress is a trajectory, and achievement is a peak you can see clearly from where you start. The three nodes also hold a structural secret: each one maps to a course in the platform (IELTS, SQL, Azure), and the gold peak maps to the Pro tier and certification achievement. No one sees this consciously — they just feel the logo has weight.
↑
Rise is literal, not decorative
The 45° trajectory line directly embodies the brand name. Every mark tells the same story: you start at navy, you move through teal, you reach gold.
●
IQ is encoded in the nodes
Data points, measurement, neural connections. The three nodes reference intelligence metrics and the Synapse Brain AI — without ever showing a brain silhouette.
⬡
Scales from 16px to billboard
At favicon size, the three nodes form an unmistakable silhouette. At large scale, the gradient line and layered rings add richness. No detail is lost at any size.
✦
Color tells the brand story
Navy = authority and trust. Teal = AI, momentum, the product's primary action color. Gold = achievement, Pro tier, exam success. Every token already exists in the design system.

09 — Guidelines
Usage rules
These rules protect the mark's legibility, brand equity, and scalability. They are not arbitrary — each rule has a reason.
Do
Use the full color version on white, cream, and gray light backgrounds for the primary brand moment.
Use the reversed (white/teal) version on any dark or navy background including the sidebar, admin panel, and dark hero sections.
Maintain clear space equal to the height of the peak node (≈7.5px at 1× scale) on all sides of the logo.
Use the icon-only version for favicons, app icons, browser tabs, and any context narrower than 120px.
Scale proportionally from the provided SVG viewBox. SVG is vector and scales perfectly to any size.
Use the monochrome version for single-color print, embroidery, engraving, or any context where color reproduction is unreliable.
Don't
Do not recolor the logo — do not replace Brand Navy, Teal, or Gold with other colors not in the brand palette.
Do not rearrange the three nodes, change their sizes, or alter the trajectory angle. The 45° line is intentional.
Do not use "Rise" and "IQ" in the same color. The two-tone split is a defining feature of the wordmark.
Do not add drop shadows, outlines, or decorative effects to the logo. Let the gold glow filter in the SVG do its work at most.
Do not place the full-color version on a mid-tone or saturated colored background — contrast will break. Use reversed or mono version instead.
Do not animate the nodes or wordmark independently. If animating, animate the entire lockup as a single unit (fade in, scale up).
